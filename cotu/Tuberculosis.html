TUBERCULOSIS

About Tuberculosis:

Tuberculosis (TB) is a bacterial infection spread through inhaling tiny droplets from the coughs or sneezes of an infected person.







    Symptoms of TB

    Typical symptoms of TB include:

  • weight loss
  • night sweats
  • high temperature
  • tiredness and fatigue
  • loss of appetite
  • swellings in the neck

What are the causes of TB?

Tuberculosis is spread through the air, which means you can only get it by breathing contaminated air. If someone who is actively sick talks, coughs, sneezes, or speaks they can spread TB.

FOOD DIET FOR TUBERCULOSIS:





Consume milk and milk products

Milk is also a great source of protein, providing strength necessary to perform day-to-day activities

Eat Pulses

A person with TB should also be encouraged to eat pulses in other forms e.g. roasted chana.

Avoid foods containing Caffine

Foods containing saturated and trans fats worsen the TB symptoms of diarrhoea and abdominal cramping and fatigue.


Eat nuts

Increased protein needs are met by including groundnut, gingelly chikki or laddoo, or dry fruit and nut mixes.

Consume fruits and fruit juices

Fruits and vegetables are an excellent source of Vitamins.

Eat Vitamin E-rich foods

Vitamin E deficiency was associated with an increased risk of progression to TB disease among HHCs of index TB cases.

Avoid: Alcohol

it increases the risk of drug toxicity.

Avoid: Carbonated drinks

Carbonated drinks are high in sugar and soda and therefore should not be consumed during TB.
